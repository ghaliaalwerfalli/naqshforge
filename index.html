Three things we
never compromise.

Every decision at Naqsh Forge is filtered through the same three principles. They are not values on a wall — they are constraints that shape every line of code we ship.

01
Privacy is non-negotiable
Your financial data never touches our servers. Never. We have no servers receiving your data. Every entry you log stays on your device, encrypted, inaccessible to anyone but you.
02
Speed earns daily use
A budget app only works if you actually use it. Three taps to log, two seconds to save. We obsess over the add-expense flow because it's the moment that determines whether the app changes your life or dies on the second page of your home screen.
03
Price should not be a barrier
We believe a well-designed financial tool should cost less than a coffee. Not $109 a year. Not $14.99 a month. One purchase. Permanent ownership. The subscription model extracts value. We'd rather earn your trust once and keep it.
How we build

The
Forge
process.

I
Identify the real friction
Most finance apps fail because they solve the wrong problem. We spend more time in the problem space than in the solution space. Orba started with one observation: every budget app requires a bank connection that most UAE expats don't want to give. That single friction point became the entire product brief.
II
Design the interaction first
We prototype the core interaction before writing any production code. For Orba, that meant: how many taps does it take to log a coffee? If the answer is more than three, start over. The UI is the product. Everything else is infrastructure.
III
Build only what's necessary
Every feature added is a feature that needs to be maintained, explained, and designed. We ask "what would we remove?" before "what would we add?" V1 of Orba has exactly the features it needs and nothing else. That restraint is the hardest engineering decision we make.
IV
Ship, listen, iterate
The real product brief arrives in your first 100 user reviews. We ship with the intent to learn. Every App Store review is a design specification for the next version. The forge is never cold — the work continues after launch.
